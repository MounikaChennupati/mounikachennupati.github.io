Semi Supervised Classification of Hyperspectral Cancer Images

Guide: Dr. Amit Sethi

      

Undergraduate Thesis

      

IIT Guwahati

      

[Report]

      

[Deliverables]

Hierarchical clustering is very important in medical imaging as it helps in visualization of levels of dissimilarity among different cells and tissues. Semi supervised clustering aims to cluster the data using the knowledge constraints, most research efforts in semi-supervised clustering use instance level must-link and cannot-link constraints, which cannot be used for hierarchical clustering since data samples are linked over different levels of hierarchy, we propose a 2-stage semi supervised clustering model which gives the necessary hierarchical structure in the data but alleviates large memory requirement problem of hierarchical clustering, there fore it is done in the 2nd stage using ultra metric transformation of dissimilarity matrix subject to triple wise relative constraints. Experimental results demonstrate the efficiency and effectiveness of our proposed method.

  • Proposed a two-stage semi-supervised clustering model which gives the necessary hierarchical structure in the data inorder to classify into four categories such as Class 0: background + other cell structures, Class1: non-goblet cell epithelium, Class2: stroma and Class3: goblet cell using partially annotated hyperspectral image data.
  • Performed dimensionality reduction using semi-supervised NMF and unlabelled sample predictions using hierarchical semi-supervised clustering.
  • Achieved accuracy rates nearer to the state of the art 72%.

[Publication] - ‘Semi-supervised Hierrarchical Clustering for Medical Hyperspectral Image classification’- In Preperation. The deliverables linked above are ready. Will be submitting to IEEE Transactions on Medical Imaging.


Rats and their Maps

Guide: Dr. Allen Cheung

      

Queensland Brain Institute

      

The University of Queensland

      

[Details]

      

[Report coming soon!]

Mounting evidence shows mammalian brains are probabilistic computers, but the specific cells involved remain elusive. Parallel research suggests that grid cells of the mammalian hippocampal formation are fundamental to spatial cognition but their diverse response properties still defy explanation. No plausible model exists which explains stable grids in darkness for twenty minutes or longer, despite being one of the first results ever published on grid cells. Similarly, no current explanation can tie together grid fragmentation and grid rescaling, which show very different forms of flexibility in grid responses when the environment is varied. Modelling efforts have largely ignored the breadth of response patterns, while also failing to account for the disastrous effects of sensory noise during spatial learning and recall, especially in darkness.

  • Developing an optimized computational tool which can build maps (allocentric representation of 3D space) using near-optimal fusion of available sensory information and biologically realistic levels of noise.
  • Implemented a Rao-Blackwellized Particle Filter for the Grid based Simultaneous Localization and Mapping (con- sidered Boundary Vector Cell (BVC) model for the same).

Note: Extension that I plan to work on soon is to solve probablistic SLAM problem by training recurrent LSTM networks to perform a set of navigation tasks that include generalization to completely novel trajectories and environments.

Sensory Integration in Zebrafish Brain

Guide: Dr. Ethan Scott

      

School of Biomedical Sciences

      

The University of Queensland

      

[Code]

The Project aims for a Comparison of Behaviour Analysis of responsive neurons for various stimuli in zebrafish tectum. The optic tectum has emerged as a tractable visuomotor transformer, in which anatomical and functional studies can allow a better understanding of how behavior is controlled by neuronal circuits. It integrates visual and other sensory inputs to produce a motor response. The zebrafish visual system is used as a model to understand neuronal circuit formation taking advantage of its small size and transparency. Since tectal responses to other modalities have remained unexplored in fish apart from visual, it is little known how inputs for non-visual sensory modalities pass through the tectum.

  • Extracted temporal sequences of Neurons in ZebraFish larvae obtained from SPIM to measure Neural Activity.
  • Implemented morphological segmentation over the gray scale data inorder to obtain ROI of neurons.
  • Compared clustering algorithms KSC & KShape over the temporal sequences of the zebrafish's brain to obtain functional network of neurons that respond to similar frequency stimuli on the auditory dataset.

Intelligent Question Answering System

Independent

      

[Accepted Paper]

Interesting aspect of NLP is that most of the tasks in it can be modeled into a Q & A model. For example, if your problem of interest is sequence modeling task (Named Entity Recognition), then the way to model it would be by giving the data as input and formulating a question What named entity tag sequence suits the best? Inorder to understand Q/A's importance, Dynamic Memory Networks have been explored optimally.

  • Implemented a new and optimised version of Dynamic Memory Networks for Text based questions on bAbi data set where 12 out of 20 tasks have achieved greater accuracy than 95%.
  • LSTMs have only passed 4/20 tasks in them. Although LSTMs exceed the performance of RNNs in sequence prediction and tasks similar to above, they fail to perform well here because of their weakly supervised nature over answers.
  • The above results confirm that the new type of neural networks equipped with episodic memory module and multiple iterations methods are beneficial for Q & A type systems.

Band Selection using Independent Component Analysis

Independent

      

[Accepted Paper]

Hyperspectral Imaging is a new and emerging technology in the field of Medical Imaging. It combines both digital imaging and spectroscopy. The "Curse of Dimensionality" in these images makes it tough to process the raw data for analysis.

  • Independent Component Analysis minimizes the higher order statistics such or Kurtosis which will essentially minimize the mutual information in the output.
  • Based on ICA, the absolute weight coefficients corresponding to each row are sorted and a band sequence is obtained. The weight matrix determines how a particular band contributes to each material in the hyperspectral image. Thus mutually independent bands can be selected from all bands.
  • From the sequence, the bands with greater absolute weight coefficients will contribute more to the independent transformations than other bands. This means that bands with higher weights will have more spectral information than the other bands.
  • This technique enables the dimensionality reduction of about 95% in a single image which can be further processed easily.
